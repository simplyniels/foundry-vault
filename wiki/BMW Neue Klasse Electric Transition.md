## What it is

BMW's Neue Klasse platform is a full-generation EV architecture (round-cell batteries, 800V charging, "Heart of Joy" central controller) being rolled out across BMW's portfolio 2026–2028. BMW's strategy is explicitly dual-track: run combustion and electric variants of the same models simultaneously, letting market adoption determine the mix rather than betting on a single technology.

## Why it matters

BMW's dual-track approach is a direct response to the risk that EV-only strategies create: VW Group's full EV pivot created product gaps and market share losses; BMW is betting that heterogeneous customer demand — especially across markets — makes a dual-track portfolio the right answer through 2030.

## Key points

- Neue Klasse battery: round cells (vs. prismatic) → +20% energy density; 800V → up to 400 kW charging; 10 min = 372 km for iX3
- Platform rollout: iX3 (2025) → i4/iX4 (2026) → iX1 (2027) → electric M3 ZA0 + electric M4 (2028-2029)
- M3 dual-track: electric ZA0 (4 motors, Heart of Joy) + combustion G84 available simultaneously
- M4: combustion maintained through decade; fully electric M4 ~2029 expected to be electric-only
- BMW M performance: 213,457 units in 2025 (14th consecutive record), 9.8% of all BMW group sales; US is largest single market (72,000+ units)
- Electric M3 vs. combustion M3: M CEO Frank van Meel confirmed the electric version will be significantly faster
- Market opportunity: BMW targeting coupé/fastback niches vacated by Audi and Mercedes
- Platform economy: 4 Series shares architecture with 3 Series and X3 — lower development cost per model
