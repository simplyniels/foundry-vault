## What it is

Positive emotion — play, curiosity, flow — is a precondition for high cognitive performance, not a reward for it. The conventional hustle-culture sequence (work hard → suffer → succeed → feel good) has causality backwards. Fredrickson's broaden-and-build theory establishes that positive states produce the cognitive wideness needed for creativity, problem-solving, and sustained deep work.

## Why it matters

If emotional state is upstream of cognitive capability, then optimising for it is legitimate performance strategy, not self-indulgence. The implication: many productivity tactics fail not because they're wrong, but because they're applied on top of a negative baseline that negates them.

## Key points

- Positive emotion broadens attention scope and builds cognitive resources (Fredrickson's broaden-and-build) — the widening is literal, not metaphorical
- The "undoing effect": positive emotions actively reverse the physiological aftermath of stress — the recovery mechanism, not just the absence of harm
- Four key hormones: endorphins (energy/motivation), serotonin (mood/sleep/wellbeing), dopamine (reward/focus), oxytocin (social/trust) — positive states run on all four
- Negative emotions trigger cortisol and adrenaline — useful short-term for threats, chronically damaging at background levels
- Sleep deprivation disrupts the same hormone systems (serotonin, dopamine) that underpin positive states — sleep is the upstream enabler (Walker)
- The diagnostic reframe: if productivity tactics aren't working, question whether the emotional precondition is met first
- The ABCs of feel-good productivity: Adventure, Bravery, Creativity as core sources of energising states (Abdaal's framework)

## Evidence across sources

## Open questions

- What is the minimum sustainable positive baseline? How much does it vary by person vs. context?
- Does the broaden-and-build effect scale to teams, or does it operate only at the individual level?
- Is the "feel good first" principle vulnerable to motivational self-deception — using it as permission to avoid hard things?
