**Summary**

Tiago Forte's system for externalising personal knowledge into a digital "second brain" — an organised, searchable, and actionable store of ideas, notes, and resources. The core method is CODE: Capture, Organise, Distill, Express. The organising framework is PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives), which sorts information by actionability rather than topic. The goal is to make your stored knowledge flow into creative output rather than pile up as an archive.

**Key points**
- Capture only information that resonates — inspiring, useful, personally important, or belief-changing
- Feynman's method: maintain a list of core questions and test every new piece of information against them
- A second brain should feel like a kitchen (organised by process/outcome), not a library (organised by topic)
- PARA: Projects (short-term with deadline), Areas (ongoing commitments), Resources (interests to explore), Archives (inactive)
- Assign every captured item to PARA by asking "which project/area/resource will this be most useful for?"
- Progressive summarisation: read → highlight key passages → bold critical ideas → write executive summary
- Intermediate packets: break projects into small, named, reusable output chunks
- Tag and search aggressively to resurface intermediate packets later
- Distillation is the core cognitive skill — compress ideas to their densest, most retrievable form

**Claude's notes**

This is the foundational Forte text. The kitchen metaphor (organised by process, not category) is a clean rebuttal to topic-based filing. The CODE/PARA system is widely adopted and cross-references heavily with the German edition ([[Nutzen Sie Ihr Zweites Gehirn]]) and the Shortform summary ([[Building a Second Brain (Shortform)]]). PARA's organising principle — actionability over category — runs through multiple sources here. Contradicts the "organise by context-of-use" principle from [[What Matters in the Age of AI Is Taste]], which is more output-focused rather than project-focused. Strong candidate for a concept article on knowledge organisation patterns.
