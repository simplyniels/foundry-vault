**Summary**

Franz Himpsl and Dirk von Gehlen's practical handbook on integrating AI into everyday life — not for IT specialists, but for anyone curious and open to shortcuts and lifehacks. The book argues that AI is already embedded in daily life (cars, medicine, offices, apps) and the real question is not whether to use it but how to use it thoughtfully. ChatGPT in particular is positioned as a persistent, versatile browser window that handles questions from any domain of life with coherent, useful answers.

**Key points**
- AI is already in everyday life — meditation apps, cars, offices, medical devices — the debate "do we want AI?" is already settled by reality
- ChatGPT differs from conventional software: it's not deterministic, it's conversational, and it adapts
- The iPhone analogy: transformative not because of specs but because it redefined interaction patterns — AI is a similar paradigm shift
- GPT-3 is far from simulating a human brain (100 trillion synapses vs. GPT-3's parameter count), but this undersells its practical utility
- AI can serve as a "thinking machine" — a persistent interlocutor for knowledge organisation and daily task management
- The path to peace of mind runs through getting things done; AI as a tool for lowering the friction on essential tasks
- AI-assisted meditation and mindfulness apps (Headspace, Calm) are early examples of personalised wellness AI

**Claude's notes**

The most lifestyle-integrated of the AI books in the vault. Himpsl and von Gehlen write for general readers, positioning AI as a practical life tool rather than a technology trend. The "thinking machine" concept — an AI as a persistent external reasoning partner — connects to the Foundry's own premise and to [[LLM Knowledge Bases (Karpathy)]]. The wellness-AI integration (Headspace, Calm algorithms) is an interesting concrete example of the AI-in-daily-life claim. Thematically thin compared to Hoffman or Kraus, but useful as a cultural-adoption snapshot. The "ChatGPT as open browser tab" metaphor is memorable.
