**Summary**

J.K. Rowling's sixth Harry Potter novel (German translation by Klaus Fritz). Harry receives private lessons from Dumbledore and begins to uncover Voldemort's past and his Horcruxes. Highlights are character moments: Dumbledore's wit, Luna's social isolation, Ron and Hermione's evolving relationship.

**Key points**
- Dumbledore's dry wit: offering a drink to Uncle Vernon with deliberate pessimism — "scheint mir optimistisch zu sein, ja geradezu töricht"
- Luna on DA meetings: "Es war, als ob ich Freunde hätte" — casually devastating note on loneliness
- Ron and Hermione's slow-burn tension finally resolved in a quiet scene over Herbology
- Slughorn's genuine delight at Hermione's talent — muggle-born excellence acknowledged warmly
- Natural talent vs. learned skill: "Ich hatte nie den Eindruck, dass ich es geschafft hätte, Potter irgendetwas beizubringen" / "Das ist ein Naturtalent!"

**Claude's notes**

More substantive highlights than the other HP volumes. Luna's "as if I had friends" line is one of Rowling's most quietly heartbreaking moments — it resonates with themes of belonging and social connection that surface in the positive psychology cluster ([[Feel-Good Productivity]] on oxytocin and social bonding). The natural talent vs. teaching paradox is a recurring theme in learning literature. Fiction processed primarily for character and dialogue texture.
