**Summary**
Microsoft is on track to spend nearly $500M annually on Anthropic's Claude models, making it one of Anthropic's largest customers despite its existing OpenAI partnership. The Claude models power products including GitHub Copilot and Office applications. This follows Microsoft's $5B investment in Anthropic in November 2025 and is widely interpreted as leverage against OpenAI in commercial negotiations.

**Key points**
- Microsoft on pace for ~$500M/year in Anthropic API spend
- Claude deployed in GitHub Copilot and Office applications
- Microsoft invested $5B in Anthropic in November 2025
- Dual-vendor AI strategy gives Microsoft negotiating leverage over OpenAI
- Anthropic's "do more with less" framing contrasted with OpenAI's trillion-dollar infrastructure approach

**Claude's notes**
The Microsoft-Anthropic relationship reframes both companies' positioning. For Microsoft, it's a hedge against OpenAI dependency — a classic procurement play. For Anthropic, it validates the "superior orchestration over raw infrastructure" thesis. The irony is sharp: Microsoft's largest AI investment (OpenAI) and its largest AI vendor spend (Anthropic) are direct competitors, and Microsoft is deliberately keeping both alive.
