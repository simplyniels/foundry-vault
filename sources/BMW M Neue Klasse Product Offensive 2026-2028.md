**Summary**
BMW M plans to launch up to 30 new or updated models by 2028 — counting facelifts, M Performance variants, body variants, CS and special editions alongside fully new models. The strategy runs combustion and electric in parallel across core lines (M3, M4, M5). The electric M3 (project ZA0) gets 4 motors and a "Heart of Joy" central controller; the M4 from ~2029 will likely be electric-only while the M3 continues dual-track.

**Key points**
- 30 new/updated BMW M products by 2028 — includes facelifts and variants, not just all-new cars
- Neue Klasse starts with iX3; M Performance versions (M60) and iX3 M follow
- M3 dual-track: electric ZA0 (4 motors, Heart of Joy) + combustion G84 run simultaneously
- M4: combustion continues to end of decade; electric M4 (~2029) likely electric-only
- M5 facelift also coming
- BMW's deliberate "both" strategy: serve EV markets and combustion markets simultaneously

**Claude's notes**
The "30 models" headline is marketing math — facelifts and variants inflate the count. The strategic content is the explicit dual-track commitment: BMW M won't abandon combustion while EV infrastructure matures. This mirrors the broader dual-track philosophy BMW uses across the main range and is a direct response to VW Group's struggles from going EV-too-fast. The electric M3 faster than combustion (as stated by M CEO Frank van Meel) is the product signal that electric is not a compromise for enthusiasts.
