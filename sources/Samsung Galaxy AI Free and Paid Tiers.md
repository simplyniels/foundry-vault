**Summary**
Samsung confirmed that Galaxy AI's base features remain permanently free, ending uncertainty around a subscription paywall. Core free tier includes writing assistant, photo tools, interpreter, transcription, Notes AI, Bixby, health assistant, Now Brief, audio eraser, and browser assistant. Advanced AI features may become paid from 2026 onwards. Complexity increases as Bixby gains Google-copied features and Perplexity integration.

**Key points**
- Base Galaxy AI features: permanently free (writing assistant, photo, interpreter, transcription, Notes AI, Bixby, Now Brief, audio eraser)
- Advanced features: possible paywall from 2026
- Perplexity integration coming in One UI 8.5 — pricing implications unclear
- Positions Samsung between Apple (AI as device feature) and subscription AI providers

**Claude's notes**
Samsung's freemium AI tiering echoes the broader industry dilemma: AI features cost money to run but free tiers drive hardware sales. The permanently-free baseline for core features is a hardware differentiation play — similar to Apple Intelligence. The premium tier opens a new SaaS revenue stream that Samsung hasn't historically had. The Perplexity integration complicates pricing because it's a third-party service with its own cost structure.
