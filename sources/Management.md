**Summary**

Frank Arnold's management guide structured around execution as the defining skill of outstanding performers. Arnold's central argument is that ideas are cheap and implementation is everything — great executives are those who get the right things done. The book addresses customer value creation, strategic adaptability, and the importance of change-orientation in a fast-moving world.

**Key points**
- Top performers are defined by execution: "They are getting the right things done"
- Ideas are easy; translating them to market outcomes is the hard part
- Value creation: the measure is the degree to which benefit and satisfaction are created for customers
- Long time horizons for new initiatives: 5–7 years before a new business line generates meaningful financial returns
- Tesla case study: builds battery factories and installs chargers while Europe debates standardised cables — execution speed as competitive moat
- "If the rate of change on the outside exceeds the rate of change inside — the end is in sight" — the survival test for organisations
- Seek change proactively; ask what opportunities are hidden in disruption
