**Summary**
Google and Samsung launched Gemini task automation on the Galaxy S26 Ultra, letting Gemini use apps (in a virtual window) on your behalf based on simple prompts. Initial focus: food delivery and rideshare apps. The system asks for clarification, shows its steps, and halts before the final irreversible action for user review. Real-world tests (ordering an Uber, ordering coffee and a croissant from Starbucks) completed successfully with minimal intervention.

**Key points**
- Gemini executes tasks in apps via a virtual window — not just suggesting, actually doing
- Starting scope: food delivery and rideshare apps
- Pattern: prompt → clarifying questions → stepwise execution → pause for review before final action
- Handles ambiguity reasonably: asked which airport, defaulted correctly on croissant temperature
- Partners: Samsung Galaxy S26 series as launch hardware

**Claude's notes**
The "pause before final action" design is the same trust pattern seen in Cowork and Dispatch: AI autonomy is maximised up to the irreversible step, then human confirms. This is becoming the standard UX pattern for agentic AI across platforms. Gemini task automation in apps closes the loop between AI response and real-world action — the same gap OpenClaw bridged via WhatsApp. The hardware-AI co-launch with Samsung signals Google is racing to get agentic features onto devices before Android becomes a commodity AI interface.
