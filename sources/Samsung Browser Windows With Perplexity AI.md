**Summary**
Samsung released its Chromium-based mobile browser for Windows, targeting seamless Samsung device continuity. The notable addition is an Agentic AI layer built with Perplexity that understands page context: it can create travel itineraries from page content, search browsing history via natural language, summarise and compare multiple open tabs, and skip to relevant sections in videos. AI features launch only in USA and South Korea initially.

**Key points**
- Samsung Internet browser exits beta on Windows; focused on phone-to-PC continuity
- Agentic AI (Perplexity-powered) understands current page context and acts on it
- Features: travel plan creation, natural-language history search, multi-tab comparison, video time-jumping
- Real phone-session continuity: same page position synced, not just bookmarks
- Samsung Pass integrated; requires Samsung account and Galaxy Book 3–6
- AI features: USA and South Korea only at launch; Germany timeline unclear

**Claude's notes**
Samsung is consistently using Perplexity as its AI partner rather than building proprietary models — a pragmatic platform bet. The browser as AI agent surface is interesting because browsing context is inherently rich: you know what the user is looking at, what they searched, what tabs are open. This is a different attack surface than assistant apps. The continuity play (same page position across devices) is understated; it removes the friction of context re-establishment when switching devices.
