**Summary**
Android's desktop mode, accessed by plugging a phone into an external monitor via USB-C to HDMI, launches a full windowed environment with taskbar, resizable/snappable windows, and spatial organisation. The author (Dhruv Bhutani, Pixel user) has replaced casual laptop use with it while travelling: email, quick edits, notes, image review. Key advantage: all files and apps already on the phone, logged in, no sync required. Limitation: not for heavy tasks; many apps still render as scaled-up mobile versions.

**Key points**
- USB-C to HDMI → phone becomes a desktop with taskbar and resizable floating windows
- Replaces casual laptop use: email, quick edits, notes, image review, light productivity
- No sync or setup: all data and logged-in apps are already on the phone
- Android achieved proper windowing system — critical for usability
- Clear limits: heavy tasks (programming, video editing) still need a laptop
- Samsung DeX users will find this familiar; now coming to Pixel hardware

**Claude's notes**
The interesting framing is "phone as your only computer for casual use": not a powerful workstation replacement, but a sufficient replacement for the 80% of laptop uses that are light. The "already logged in, no sync" advantage is underappreciated — the mental overhead of getting a second device set up is the hidden cost of travel computing. The windowing system quality is the key technical unlock that makes this viable; it wasn't there two years ago.
