**Summary**

Brian Merchant's investigative history of the iPhone — its development inside Apple, the global supply chain behind it, and its transformation of modern life. The book traces the device from concept to global impact.

**Key points**
- "The iPhone isn't just a tool; it's the foundational instrument of modern life" — Merchant's core thesis

**Claude's notes**

Single highlight — the thesis statement. The claim is worth keeping as a framing anchor: the iPhone is not a product category but a civilisational infrastructure. This framing appears in [[Wie KI Dein Leben Besser Macht]] (the iPhone analogy for AI: transformative not because of specs but because it redefined interaction patterns). The parallel is explicit and the two sources reinforce each other on technological paradigm shifts.
