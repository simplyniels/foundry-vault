**Summary**
Samsung is evolving Galaxy AI from a single-assistant experience into a multi-agent orchestration platform. Internal research found nearly 8 in 10 users regularly use more than two AI agents. The first integration is Perplexity ("Hey Plex"), deeply embedded at the OS level across first-party apps (Gallery, Notes, Calendar, Clock, Reminders), with system-level agent support coming for the Galaxy S26 series.

**Key points**
- 80% of Samsung users regularly use 2+ AI agents — driving platform-level orchestration
- Agent integration at OS level: no app-switching required, agents share context naturally
- Perplexity embedded as first new agent; invoke via "Hey Plex" or side button
- Plex handles multi-step workflows within Samsung's first-party apps and select third-party apps
- Galaxy AI positions itself as the orchestrator — "bringing different forms of AI into a cohesive experience"
- Upgraded Bixby (One UI 8.5) can change system settings via natural language; adds real-time web search

**Claude's notes**
Samsung's framing — Galaxy AI as orchestrator — echoes the same architectural shift happening in software development (Cherny's parallel agents) and in productivity tools (Cowork, Dispatch). The AI model itself becomes a background service; the differentiator shifts to orchestration quality and context continuity. Samsung's OS-level integration is the hardware/platform play that cloud-native AI providers can't easily replicate.
