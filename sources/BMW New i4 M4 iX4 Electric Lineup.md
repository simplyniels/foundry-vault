**Summary**
BMW is reviving the 4 Series with a new generation including both combustion and electric (i4) variants, adding a new iX4 SUV, and developing an electric M4 alongside a combustion M4. The electric i4 will use a ~108 kWh battery for ~805 km range. BMW is exploiting a market gap as Audi and Mercedes pull back from coupé/fastback niches.

**Key points**
- New 4 Series generation: shares platform with 3 Series and X3, keeping development costs low
- Electric i4: ~108 kWh battery, ~805 km range
- Electric M4 in development alongside combustion M4 — dual-track strategy
- iX4: new electric SUV, seen as spy shots multiple times
- BMW targeting coupé/fastback niches vacated by Audi and Mercedes

**Claude's notes**
BMW's dual-track (combustion + electric) strategy on the same model lines is a hedge against EV adoption uncertainty. The cost-sharing with 3 Series/X3 makes the business case viable even at lower volumes. The iX4 fills the electric coupé-SUV segment that Tesla's Model Y and Audi Q4 have validated.
