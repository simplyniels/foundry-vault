**Summary**

Chris Bailey's book on deep attention management, arguing that deliberate focus on worthy tasks — "hyperfocus" — is the highest-leverage productivity habit. Bailey distinguishes between managing time and managing attention: time is finite and fixed, but attention is the variable that determines what gets done and how well.

**Key points**
- Awareness of what you are working on is the prerequisite for improving it
- Gains come from making everything worth your time — and from identifying what isn't
- Extra conscious hours can be extracted from a day by deliberately tracking and choosing what occupies attention
