**Summary**
Day One, the journalling app, added AI features under a new "Gold" subscription tier (€79.99/year). The centrepiece is "Daily Chat": instead of writing journal entries, you tell the AI about your day (text or voice), it asks follow-up questions, and generates a formatted entry in your personal tone. All AI inputs are end-to-end encrypted, not stored or used for model training. The company is also evaluating on-device AI to eliminate external server dependency entirely.

**Key points**
- Three tiers: free, Silver, Gold (€79.99/year for AI features)
- Daily Chat: conversational journal creation — talk about your day, AI generates the entry
- Also includes: entry summaries, automatic title suggestions, image generation
- E2E encryption; no data stored; not used for training — privacy-first AI framing
- On-device AI under consideration to remove external server dependency
- Currently iPhone and iPad only

**Claude's notes**
Day One's implementation of AI in a deeply personal context (private diary) is a useful case study in how to do AI integration responsibly: end-to-end encryption, no training data, on-device evaluation. The Daily Chat feature is interesting as a form of knowledge extraction — the AI becomes a structured interviewer for your own life, which lowers the barrier to entry for regular journalling. The privacy engineering here (E2E + no storage) should be the baseline for any AI touching personal data.
