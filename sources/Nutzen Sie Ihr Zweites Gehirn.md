**Summary**

The German edition of Tiago Forte's Building a Second Brain, co-adapted by Jordan Wegberg. Covers the PARA framework (Projekte, Areale, Ressourcen, Archiv) in depth, the principle of organising by actionability rather than category, and progressive summarisation as the core distillation technique. The most detailed of the three BASB sources in the vault, with extended examples and Forte's personal backstory on how PARA emerged from frustration with complex filing systems.

**Key points**
- Sorting by project was Forte's natural fallback — PARA formalises what already worked
- PARA is universal: apply the same four categories across apps, file systems, and cloud storage
- Information sorted by actionability (Projects > Areas > Resources > Archive), not by type or origin
- The kitchen metaphor: organise by destination (where info is going), not by origin (where it came from)
- "Rasch bewegen, leicht berühren" — move fast, touch lightly; avoid perfectionism in organisation
- Progressive summarisation: four layers — original note → highlights → bold key phrases → executive summary in your own words
- Digital workspaces must support the desired mindset; clutter is a cognitive tax
- The note paradox: more notes → harder to find them → paralysis or tool-switching
- PARA turns cleanup from a never-ending task into a goal-directed activity done alongside real work
- Areas have standards, not end dates; they hold ongoing commitments like health, finances, family
- Archive is the preserve, not the trash: inactive items stay retrievable

**Claude's notes**

This is the richest and most detailed BASB source in the vault. The extended PARA examples (real folder structures from Forte's own setup) make the system concrete in a way the other versions don't. The "organise by destination" framing is sharper here than in the English original. The meta-point — that digital environments either support or undermine thinking, and should be designed accordingly — bridges into the AI-context-feeding argument in [[What Matters in the Age of AI Is Taste]].
