**Summary**

Yvonne Kraus's introductory guide to ChatGPT for general audiences. Explains what large language models are (transformer-based neural networks trained on text) and how to interact with them effectively — through iterative conversation, specific context-giving, and feedback loops. The core insight is that AI doesn't understand, it predicts; and good results come from treating the session as a dialogue, not a command prompt.

**Key points**
- ChatGPT uses transformer models that process sequential data (text) and predict contextually appropriate outputs
- Deep learning + neural networks: multiple layers recognise increasingly abstract patterns in training data
- GPT = Generative Pre-trained Transformer — it generates text, pre-trained on massive corpora
- AI distinguishes "statistically probable" from "statistically improbable" — not "true" from "false"
- AI does not reason; it pattern-matches. It cannot count characters in words reliably (not a logical operation)
- Chatbot results are non-reproducible — same prompt, different session → different output
- Context is everything: give the AI text to read (delimited with ### ... ###) before asking questions about it
- "Ask the AI what it needs": prompt it to interview you before answering — a metacognitive move that improves outputs
- Custom Instructions (ChatGPT Plus): two persistent prompts of up to 1,500 characters each, loaded into every session
- Thread context matters: stay in the same conversation when working on a topic — the AI "remembers" the thread

**Claude's notes**

A solid entry-level technical explanation alongside practical usage tips. The "probability not truth" framing for LLM behaviour is a useful corrective to anthropomorphisation, echoing Hoffman's more technical take in [[Ideen Auf Knopfdruck]]. The Custom Instructions feature and thread-continuity advice are operationally useful for building effective AI habits. The "feed the AI a text to read" technique maps directly onto the context-window argument in [[What Matters in the Age of AI Is Taste]]. Three AI-prompting sources now share the conversation-as-method thesis: this source, [[Clever Gefragt, Prompt Gelöst]], and [[Wie KI Dein Leben Besser Macht]].
