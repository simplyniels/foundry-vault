**Summary**
BMW M sold 213,457 vehicles in 2025, its 14th consecutive sales record and a 3.3% increase over the prior year. Mercedes-AMG sold 145,000 units (up 7%) — a significant gap. The USA is the largest market for both brands; BMW M accounts for 9.8% of all BMW group sales. The electric BMW M3 (project ZA0) is in development and expected to be significantly faster than the combustion version.

**Key points**
- BMW M: 213,457 units in 2025, 14th consecutive record, +3.3% year-on-year
- Mercedes-AMG: 145,000 units, +7% — gap of 68,000+ units
- USA largest market for both: BMW M sells 72,000+ units there
- BMW M now represents 9.8% of all BMW group sales — nearly 1 in 10 new BMWs
- Electric M3 (ZA0): 4 electric motors, new "Heart of Joy" central controller, will exceed combustion M3 performance

**Claude's notes**
BMW M's dominance over AMG has been consistent for years; the gap is widening despite AMG's faster growth rate, which suggests BMW's absolute scale advantage compounds. The nearly 10% internal share is remarkable for a performance sub-brand — it means M Performance is mainstream BMW, not a niche. The electric M3 faster than combustion is a proof-of-concept that EV performance can exceed ICE in a brand where performance IS the product.
