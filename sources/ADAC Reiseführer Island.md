**Summary**

ADAC's practical travel guide to Iceland by Bernd Bierbaum. Covers top attractions (Hallgrímskirkja, Þingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss, Jökulsárlón), Reykjavík city centre on foot, restaurant and café recommendations, and regional highlights. Less cultural essay than Magnusson's book; more structured planning tool with opening hours, tips, and route logic.

**Key points**
- Reykjavík's centre is walkable — all key sights within walking distance of old harbour and Tjörnin lake
- Hallgrímskirkja: 74m church tower, Basalt-column-inspired facade, lift to top for panoramic views
- Harpa concert hall: Olafur Elíasson facade, coloured glass panels mirroring Northern Lights in winter
- Laugavegur: Reykjavík's main street, boutiques, cafés, restaurants — open late
- Café Mokka: historic café institution since the 1960s, good coffee, famous waffles
- Þingvellir: site of one of the world's oldest parliaments (930 AD) AND the tectonic plate boundary
- The Blue Lagoon: industrial geothermal wastewater repurposed as spa; kieselsäure gives the electric-blue colour
- Perlan: rotating glass dome with city panorama, museum on Iceland's natural phenomena
- Best waterfalls: Gullfoss (Golden Circle), Skógafoss, Dettifoss (most powerful in Europe)
- Glacier lagoon Jökulsárlón: icebergs calving from Vatnajökull glacier tongues
- Seltjarnarnes peninsula: best aurora viewing spot within Reykjavík in winter
- Two thirds of Icelanders live in the Reykjavík region
