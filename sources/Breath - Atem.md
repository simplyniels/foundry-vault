**Summary**

James Nestor's investigation into the science of breathing, drawing on ancient practices and modern pulmonology to argue that most contemporary humans breathe incorrectly — and that this is causing a cascade of preventable chronic conditions. The core claim: 90% of people breathe sub-optimally; learning to breathe differently can alleviate or reverse asthma, anxiety, ADHD, psoriasis, and other modern diseases.

**Key points**
- 90% of people breathe incorrectly; this has worsened since industrialisation
- Breathing patterns affect body weight, overall health, and can influence anxiety, attention, and skin conditions
- Chronic nasal congestion affects 40% of humanity — mouth breathing compounds problems
- Dental anthropology: wisdom tooth extraction narrows the palate and raises risk of sleep apnea
- Modern diet has softened jaw musculature and narrowed airways over generations
- Sudarshan Kriya breathing practice: produces genuine calm, better sleep, reduced daily friction
- Breathing rhythm can be deliberately varied — different rhythms produce measurably different physiological states
